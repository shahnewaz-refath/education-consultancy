Why study Lithuania

Lithuania is a country and the southernmost of Europe’s Baltic states, a former Soviet bloc nation bordering Poland, Latvia and Belarus. Its capital, Vilnius, near Belarus' border, is known for its medieval Old Town. It also has Gothic, Renaissance and baroque architecture, and 18th-century cathedral built on a pagan temple site. Hilltop Gediminas' Tower, a symbol of the city and the nation, offers sweeping views.

A degree from Lithuania will help you gain a job all over the world. Lithuania is one of the best places to pursue higher education. It also has many places for tourist attractions to occupy your free time. Living cost is less comparable to other EU countries.

Here are some major requirements to study in Lithuania:

  • Qualifications and Grades.
  • Entrance tests.(For some university)
  • Language proficiency. (IELTS/TOEFL/GRE or native language etc.)
  • ID documents.
  • Experience and references.(If required)
  • Interviews.
  • Additional entry requirements.(If required)

Some advantages of Lithuania

Economical Country

The cost of living and studying in Lithuania is lower than the other western countries, thus making it a great destination for study, business or tourism.

The education is free.

People who are expatriating to Lithuania with their kids should know that education in Lithuania is mostly free. Students who had good grades in the high school get to study in universities and colleges for free, while the ones with below average scores need to pay a little. However, one thing is for sure – students in Lithuania graduate from universities without huge debts for the rest of their lives.

Mobility Opportunities

The higher education institutions of Lithuania are actively involved and are part of several international programmes and agreements. They are a part of the Erasmus Mundus programme, the Fulbright programme, and more. The institutions keep on building links with the higher education institutions abroad, thus resulting in exchange programmes/projects. The students, researchers and teachers are a part of these exchange programmes.

Great Learning Environment

The higher education institutions of Lithuania comprise of excellent research literature, computing and technology equipments. Students and researchers are provided a superb learning and research environment. The lectures and classes are being taken by some of the highly qualified faculty members.
